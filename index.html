About the Simulation

This simulation uses the WebGL library ThreeJS to draw and animate the solar system geometry on an html5 canvas.
I tried my best to model this solar system to accurate proportions to stay consistent with the real solar system to both make it
more interesting to look at and to get a greater sense of the immensity of the solar system we find ourselves living in.
Below I outline the data used to create the simulation.

The Size of the Planets

To get E we use the suns diameter / 54.5. The suns radius is 109 times greater than earths, but we use half.
The proportions.
E = Earth's diameter

  • Mercury: 38% the size of Earth (E * 0.38)
  • Venus: 95% the size of Earth (E * 0.95)
  • Mars: 53% the size of Earth (E * 0.53)
  • Juptier: 1120% the size of Earth (E * 11.2)
  • Saturn: 945% the size of Earth (E * 9.45)
  • Uranus: 400% the size of Earth (E * 4.0)
  • Neptune: 388% the size of Earth (E * 3.88)

The Distance of the Planets from the Sun

This calculation uses the average distance of the planets from the sun (S) in millions of Kilometers (mKM) scaled down by 10.
* In the code, 5.1 is added to each calculation to offset from the edge of the sun rather than the center, noted as D.

  • Mercury: 57mKM (S + 5.7 + D)
  • Venus: 108mKM (S + 10.8 + D)
  • Earth: 149mKM (S + 14.9 + D)
  • Mars: 228mKM (S + 22.8 + D)
  • Juptier: 780mKM (S + 78.0 + D)
  • Saturn: 1437mKM (S + 143.7 + D)
  • Uranus: 2871mKM (S + 287.1 + D)
  • Neptune: 4530mKM (S + 453.0 + D)

The Orbit Speed of the Planets

The orbital velocity in kilometers per second (km/s) scaled down by 10

  • Mercury: 47.87 km/s (4.787)
  • Venus: 35.02 km/s (3.502)
  • Earth: 29.48 km/s (2.948)
  • Mars: 24.077 km/s (2.4077)
  • Juptier: 13.07 km/s (1.307)
  • Saturn: 9.69 km/s (0.969)
  • Uranus: 6.81 km/s (0.681)
  • Neptune: 5.43 km/s (0.543)
